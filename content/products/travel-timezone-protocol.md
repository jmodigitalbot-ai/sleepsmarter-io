## Why Most Travel Sleep Advice Fails You

Most advice tells you to "stay hydrated" and "avoid caffeine on the plane."

That's fine. It's also useless for the actual problem: your circadian clock is anchored to your home timezone, and it doesn't care that you have a 9am meeting in London.

The Travel & Timezone Protocol is built around one principle:

> **Your body doesn't know what time it is. But it does know light, temperature, food, and movement. Those are your levers.**

This protocol gives you the exact sequence to pull those levers — before, during, and after travel — so your clock resets in 48 hours instead of 7 days.

## PART 1: The Jet Lag Calculator

Before you travel, calculate your reset difficulty using this formula:

**Timezone Difference × Direction Multiplier = Reset Score**

| Direction | Multiplier | Why |
|-----------|-----------|-----|
| Eastward (e.g., US → Europe) | × 1.5 | You're shortening your day — harder to advance your clock |
| Westward (e.g., Europe → US) | × 1.0 | You're extending your day — easier to delay your clock |

**Reset Score → Recovery Time:**
- 1–4: 1–2 days
- 5–8: 2–4 days (use the 48-Hour Reset below)
- 9+: 4–7 days (use the 48-Hour Reset + Anchor Protocol)

**Example:**
New York → London = 5 hours east × 1.5 = **7.5 → 2–4 days without intervention**

With this protocol: **48 hours.**

## PART 2: The 48-Hour Reset Sequence

### Phase 1: Pre-Flight (24–48 Hours Before Departure)

**If traveling EAST:**
- Shift your bedtime 30 minutes earlier each night for 2 nights before you fly
- Move your morning light exposure 30 minutes earlier each day
- Eat your last meal 30 minutes earlier than usual

**If traveling WEST:**
- Stay up 30 minutes later each of the 2 nights before you fly
- Delay your morning light exposure by 30 minutes each day
- Push your last meal 30 minutes later than usual

**Both directions:**
- Get bright light exposure for 15–20 minutes within 30 minutes of waking (natural sunlight or a 10,000 lux lamp)
- Avoid alcohol for 48 hours before departure — it fragments sleep architecture at the worst possible time

---

### Phase 2: In-Flight

**Set your watch to destination time immediately when you board.**

This is psychological anchoring. Your brain starts processing the shift.

| Destination Time | What to Do on the Plane |
|-----------------|------------------------|
| It's "night" at destination | Wear eye mask. Use earplugs or noise-canceling headphones. Decline meal service if it conflicts with destination night. Sleep. |
| It's "day" at destination | Stay awake. Keep window shades open if daytime in flight path. Eat normally. Move around every 60–90 minutes. |

**Hydration rule:** 8oz of water per hour of flight. Airplane cabin humidity is ~10–20% (desert-level). Dehydration amplifies every jet lag symptom.

**Avoid:**
- Alcohol (delays adaptation by up to 2x)
- Sleeping pills unless crossing 8+ time zones (they sedate, they don't reset)
- Blue light screens within 60 minutes of destination "bedtime"

---

### Phase 3: First 48 Hours at Destination

**This is where most people fail.** They let themselves "take it easy" — sleeping in, napping long, avoiding sunlight. That locks in the wrong timezone.

**Day 1 Arrival:**

*If arriving in the morning/afternoon:*
- Get outside within 30 minutes of landing. 15–20 minutes of direct sunlight, ideally between 8–10am destination time.
- Stay awake until 9:30–10pm local time, even if exhausted
- Eat meals at local mealtimes — this is your second strongest clock signal after light
- Keep dinner light (heavy meals spike core body temperature, delaying sleep onset)

*If arriving at night:*
- Take 0.5mg melatonin (see Part 3) 60 minutes before local bedtime
- Keep room temperature at 65–68°F / 18–20°C
- Total darkness — pack a quality sleep mask for this reason

**Day 2:**
- Morning sunlight again, 8–10am local time, 20+ minutes
- No naps over 20 minutes
- Light exercise at local 5–7pm (body temperature raise → faster adaptation)
- Target bedtime: 10pm local

By end of Day 2, you should feel 80–90% adapted.

## PART 3: The Melatonin Timing Chart

Most people use melatonin wrong. They take 5–10mg at bedtime. That's a sleep sedative dose, not a clock signal.

**For jet lag, you want a clock signal — not sedation.**

**The right dose: 0.5mg – 1mg**
**The right timing:** depends on direction of travel

### Eastward Travel (Advancing Your Clock)

| Day | When to Take It |
|-----|----------------|
| Day 1 at destination | 60 minutes before your TARGET local bedtime |
| Day 2 | Same time as Day 1 |
| Day 3 (if needed) | Same time, or skip if fully adapted |

*Example: Flying NYC → London. Target bedtime 10pm London time. Take 0.5mg at 9pm.*

### Westward Travel (Delaying Your Clock)

Melatonin is less critical for westward travel (your body adapts more easily). Use it only if:
- You're crossing 6+ time zones westward
- You're having trouble falling asleep at local bedtime

Take: 0.5mg, 30–60 minutes before local target bedtime on Day 1 only.

### Melatonin Timing Don'ts
- ❌ Don't take it in the morning (can shift your clock the wrong direction)
- ❌ Don't take more than 1mg (higher doses = sedation, not clock reset)
- ❌ Don't combine with alcohol
- ❌ Don't use if you're staying fewer than 3 days (not worth forcing adaptation if you're leaving again)

## PART 4: The Short-Trip Rule (3 Days or Less)

For trips of 3 days or fewer, a full reset isn't worth it. Your body won't fully adapt anyway.

**Instead, use the Home Anchor Strategy:**

- Keep your sleep/wake times as close to home timezone as possible
- Schedule important meetings during your "peak hours" in your home timezone
- Use blackout curtains and earplugs to block conflicting light/sound cues
- Take 0.5mg melatonin at your *home* bedtime (not local) to reinforce the anchor

This is harder socially but easier biologically. Choose accordingly.

## PART 5: The Frequent Traveler Maintenance Stack

If you travel more than once per month, chronic circadian disruption accumulates. Add these to your baseline:

**Magnesium glycinate** — 200–400mg taken 60 minutes before bed. Supports GABA receptor function (the brakes on your nervous system). Particularly valuable when your clock is disrupted. *(Doctor's Best brand, available on Amazon)*

**Morning light anchor** — Every single day, including travel days: 10–20 minutes of bright light within 30 minutes of waking. This is the single highest-leverage habit for clock stability. Do it before you check your phone.

**Consistent wake time** — Within 30 minutes of your target time, every day. Even on weekends. Even after a red-eye. The consistency of the wake signal matters more than the sleep duration.

**Temperature at night** — 65–68°F / 18–20°C in your sleeping environment. Book hotels with controllable AC. This isn't comfort preference — it's a clock signal.
