# sleepsmarter.io CRO Audit Checklist

Purpose: turn SEO traffic into leads, affiliate clicks, and `/sleep-reset` buyers instead of publishing traffic-only articles.

Core path:

organic/search visitor → useful answer → trust → email/quiz/lead magnet → `/sleep-reset` or affiliate click → revenue

## Publish Gate

A new or updated article is not done unless these pass:

1. Search intent match
2. Clear next step
3. Problem-specific CTA
4. Internal link ladder
5. Monetization path

Scoring:
- 90-100: strong, publish-ready
- 75-89: acceptable, fix key leaks soon
- 60-74: weak, likely leaks readers
- <60: do not publish without CRO revision

## Article Scorecard

Score each item 0-4.

### Intent and First Screen
- [ ] Title matches a real search/problem pattern
- [ ] Meta description promises a concrete answer
- [ ] Intro answers the query quickly
- [ ] First screen makes the reader feel understood
- [ ] Article states what the reader will know or do by the end
- [ ] Reading level is simple

### CTA Strategy
- [ ] Early CTA appears after first useful answer or first major section
- [ ] Mid-article CTA appears at a natural decision point
- [ ] Bottom CTA matches article conclusion
- [ ] CTA language is problem-specific
- [ ] CTA offers immediate value, not vague subscription language
- [ ] CTA does not interrupt before trust is earned

### Cluster and Internal Links
- [ ] Links to 4+ relevant sleepsmarter articles when possible
- [ ] Links use descriptive anchor text
- [ ] Cluster path is logical: problem → cause → fix → offer
- [ ] No obsolete or file-number slugs
- [ ] No orphan article risk

### Monetization
- [ ] Affiliate links only appear where buying intent exists
- [ ] Affiliate recommendations include best for, avoid if, or buying guidance
- [ ] `/sleep-reset` bridge appears where intent fits
- [ ] Lead magnet idea is identified if the article has traffic potential
- [ ] CTA maps to reader intent stage: TOFU, MOFU, BOFU

### Proof, Trust, and Usefulness
- [ ] Claims are evidence-backed or clearly practical
- [ ] Advice is specific enough to act on tonight
- [ ] No generic wellness fluff
- [ ] FAQ handles common objections or follow-up questions
- [ ] Schema/FAQ opportunity is noted if useful

### Mobile and Scanability
- [ ] Short paragraphs
- [ ] Scannable H2/H3s
- [ ] Lists/tables used where they reduce cognitive load
- [ ] No walls of text before first useful answer
- [ ] CTA blocks are readable on mobile

## CTA Library by Cluster

### 3AM Waking / Cortisol

Early:
> Still waking up at 3AM? Take the 2-minute Sleep Type check and find the fix that matches your pattern.

Mid:
> If your wake-up time is predictable, your fix should be too. Get the 3AM wake-up checklist before tonight.

Bottom:
> Want the full reset plan? Start with the 7-day Sleep Reset and rebuild your wind-down from the alarm backward.

### Supplements

Early:
> Not sure if magnesium, melatonin, L-theanine, or valerian fits your problem? Get the simple sleep supplement timing chart first.

Mid:
> Before you buy another bottle, match the supplement to the actual sleep problem.

Bottom:
> If supplements keep giving mixed results, fix the schedule and wind-down first. Start the 7-day Sleep Reset.

### Sleep Tracker / Orthosomnia

Early:
> Your sleep tracker might be making your sleep worse. Use the tracker detox checklist before chasing another score.

Mid:
> If your score ruins your morning, your tracker is no longer neutral. Try a 7-day reset without score-chasing.

Bottom:
> Keep the data if it helps. Drop the obsession if it doesn't. Start with the Sleep Reset protocol.

### Bedtime / Sleep Calculator

Early:
> Want your ideal bedtime without guessing? Use the sleep calculator, then check what your schedule says about your sleep type.

Mid:
> Bedtime is only half the equation. Your wake-up routine decides whether that sleep actually pays off.

Bottom:
> If your calculated bedtime still doesn't fix your mornings, the problem is probably your wake routine. Use the 7-day Sleep Reset.

### Mattress / Product / Affiliate

Early:
> Buying gear will not fix the wrong sleep schedule, but the right product can remove friction. Here is what actually matters.

Mid:
> Before you compare prices, decide what problem the product needs to solve: temperature, pressure, motion, noise, or consistency.

Bottom:
> If your setup is decent but your sleep still feels broken, start with the routine before buying more gear.
