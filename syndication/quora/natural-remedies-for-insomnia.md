## 1. For Sleep Onset Insomnia (Trouble Falling Asleep)

### Magnesium Supplementation
**How it works:** Magnesium is involved in hundreds of biochemical reactions, including those that regulate sleep. It helps activate the parasympathetic nervous system (the "rest and digest" system).

**Effective forms:**
- Magnesium glycinate (most bioavailable, least likely to cause digestive issues)
- Magnesium citrate (good absorption, may have laxative effect)
- Magnesium L-threonate (crosses blood-brain barrier, good for cognitive aspects)

**Dosage:** 200-400 mg about 30 minutes before bed
**Note:** Start with lower dose and increase gradually

### Tart Cherry Juice
**How it works:** Natural source of melatonin and contains compounds that may increase tryptophan availability.

**Protocol:** 8 ounces about 1-2 hours before bed
**Tip:** Look for 100% tart cherry juice without added sugars

### Valerian Root
**How it works:** May increase GABA (a calming neurotransmitter) in the brain.

**Forms:** Tea, tincture, or capsules
**Timing:** Take 30-60 minutes before bed
**Caution:** Can interact with some medications; not recommended for long-term daily use

## 2. For Sleep Maintenance Insomnia (Waking Up During the Night)

### Glycine
**How it works:** An amino acid that may help lower core body temperature and improve sleep quality.

**Dosage:** 3 grams before bed
**Form:** Powder (mix with water) or capsules
**Benefits:** Also shown to reduce daytime sleepiness

### L-Theanine
**How it works:** An amino acid found in green tea that promotes relaxation without sedation.

**Dosage:** 100-400 mg before bed
**Good for:** People with anxiety-related insomnia
**Note:** Can also be taken during the day for anxiety

### Apigenin (Chamomile Extract)
**How it works:** A flavonoid that binds to GABA receptors, similar to some anti-anxiety medications (but much milder).

**Forms:** Strong chamomile tea or standardized extract
**Dosage:** 1-2 cups of strong tea or 50 mg extract

## 3. For Circadian Rhythm Disruption

### Light Therapy
**For delayed sleep phase (night owl):** Bright light therapy within 30 minutes of waking
**For advanced sleep phase (early bird):** Bright light therapy in the evening

**Device:** Light therapy lamp (10,000 lux)
**Duration:** 20-30 minutes daily
**Consistency:** Most important factor

### Melatonin (Used Correctly)
**Common mistake:** Taking too much (0.3-1 mg is often sufficient)
**Timing:** 30-60 minutes before desired bedtime
**Use case:** Best for circadian rhythm issues, not general sleep aid

## 4. Behavioral & Lifestyle Approaches (Most Important)

### Sleep Restriction Therapy
**Paradoxical but effective:** Temporarily restricting time in bed can consolidate sleep.

**How to:**
1. Calculate your average total sleep time
2. Limit time in bed to that amount + 30 minutes
3. Maintain consistent wake time
4. Gradually increase time in bed as sleep improves

### Stimulus Control
**Principle:** Reassociate bed with sleep (not wakefulness)

**Rules:**
1. Bed is only for sleep and sex
2. If not asleep in 20 minutes, get up
3. Return only when sleepy
4. Repeat as needed

### Cognitive Behavioral Therapy for Insomnia (CBT-I)
**Gold standard:** Addresses thoughts and behaviors that perpetuate insomnia

**Components:**
- Sleep education
- Stimulus control
- Sleep restriction
- Cognitive restructuring
- Relaxation training

## 5. Dietary Considerations

### Timing Matters
- Last meal: 2-3 hours before bed
- Protein-rich snack if needed: Greek yogurt, nuts, cheese
- Avoid: Heavy, spicy, or high-fat foods close to bedtime

### Helpful Nutrients
- **Tryptophan:** Turkey, eggs, cheese, nuts, seeds
- **Vitamin B6:** Chickpeas, salmon, potatoes, bananas
- **Calcium:** Dairy, leafy greens, fortified foods

## What Doesn't Work (Common Myths)

### Alcohol
**Reality:** While it may help you fall asleep initially, it disrupts sleep architecture and causes middle-of-the-night awakenings.

### Excessive Exercise Before Bed
**Reality:** Intense exercise raises core temperature and cortisol, which can interfere with sleep onset for some people.

### "Natural" Sleep Aids with Undisclosed Ingredients
**Warning:** Some "natural" products contain undisclosed pharmaceuticals. Buy from reputable sources.

## Safety Considerations

### Consult a Healthcare Provider If:
- You're pregnant or breastfeeding
- You have liver or kidney issues
- You're taking medications (especially blood thinners, diabetes meds, or antidepressants)
- You have a medical condition

### Start Low, Go Slow
- Begin with one supplement at a time
- Start with lowest effective dose
- Give it 2-4 weeks to assess effectiveness
- Keep a sleep diary to track results
