## What to Look For in a Mattress for Back Pain {#what-to-look-for}

Choosing the right mattress for back pain isn't just about firmness — though that matters too. Here's what to consider:

### Firmness Level

Contrary to popular belief, a rock-hard mattress isn't best for back pain. Research published in *The Lancet* found that **medium-firm mattresses** (around 6-7 on a 10-point scale) reduced back pain more effectively than firm mattresses.

- **Side sleepers:** Medium (5-6) allows hips and shoulders to sink in
- **Back sleepers:** Medium-firm (6-7) supports the lumbar curve
- **Stomach sleepers:** Firm (7-8) prevents the pelvis from sinking

### Spinal Alignment

Your mattress should keep your spine in a neutral position — a straight line from your neck to your tailbone when viewed from behind. If your hips sink too deep or your shoulders don't compress enough, your spine curves unnaturally, leading to morning pain.

### Zoned Support

Many modern mattresses feature **zoned support systems** with firmer sections under the lumbar region and softer areas for shoulders. This targeted approach helps maintain spinal alignment regardless of sleep position.

### Materials

- **Memory foam:** Excellent pressure relief, good for side sleepers, can trap heat
- **Latex:** Responsive, naturally cooling, durable, good for combination sleepers (for a pure latex build, see the [Latex Mattress Factory Organic Latex Essential](https://tidd.ly/4bbF0B9))
- **Innerspring/hybrid:** Excellent support, good airflow, better edge support
- **Coils:** Targeted support, good for heavier individuals

### Your Body Weight

Body weight affects how deeply you sink into a mattress:
- **Under 130 lbs:** Consider softer options (may not compress firmer mattresses enough)
- **130-230 lbs:** Most mattresses work well; follow sleep position guidelines
- **Over 230 lbs:** Look for firmer options with reinforced support layers

Understanding [how sleep cycles work](/articles/how-sleep-cycles-work) can also help you appreciate why proper spinal alignment matters — you need to reach deep sleep stages to allow your muscles and tissues to recover.
