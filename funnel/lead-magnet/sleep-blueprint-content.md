## Your Sleep Profile

Right now, you're probably leaving quality sleep on the table ‚Äî and it's costing you more than you think. Poor sleep doesn't just make you tired. It affects your focus, your mood, your metabolism, and even how long you live.

But here's what most people get wrong: **better sleep isn't about sleeping more. It's about sleeping smarter.**

The sleep calculator you just used measures something most people have never heard of ‚Äî *sleep cycles*. Each cycle lasts about 90 minutes and takes your brain through four distinct stages. Wake up in the middle of a cycle, and you feel groggy no matter how many hours you slept. Wake up at the END of a cycle, and you feel refreshed ‚Äî even with less total sleep.

That's the foundation of everything in this blueprint.

## Your Ideal Sleep Schedule

Based on sleep science research, here's how to calculate your perfect schedule:

### Step 1: Lock Your Wake Time

Your wake time is your anchor. It should be the same every day ‚Äî yes, including weekends. Pick a time that works for your life and commit to it for at least 7 days.

### Step 2: Count Backwards in 90-Minute Cycles

From your wake time, count backwards in 90-minute blocks. You want 5-6 complete cycles per night.

**If you wake at 6:00 AM:**
- 6 cycles (9 hours): Bedtime at **9:00 PM**
- 5 cycles (7.5 hours): Bedtime at **10:30 PM** ‚Üê Sweet spot for most adults
- 4 cycles (6 hours): Bedtime at **12:00 AM** ‚Üê Minimum for function

**If you wake at 7:00 AM:**
- 6 cycles: Bedtime at **10:00 PM**
- 5 cycles: Bedtime at **11:30 PM** ‚Üê Sweet spot
- 4 cycles: Bedtime at **1:00 AM**

### Step 3: Add 15 Minutes

Most people take 10-20 minutes to fall asleep. Add 15 minutes to your bedtime. So if your target is 10:30 PM, get in bed at **10:15 PM**.

### Your Golden Window

That 15-minute window before your target sleep time is critical. This is when you should be:
- In bed
- Lights off
- Eyes closed
- Breathing slowly

Miss this window and you may have to wait another 90 minutes for the next cycle alignment.

## The 7-Day Sleep Reset Protocol (Preview)

This protocol has helped thousands of people reset their sleep in just one week. Here's the roadmap:

### Day 1: Anchor Your Wake Time
Set one alarm. Wake up at the same time regardless of when you fell asleep. This is the hardest day but the most important. Your body needs a consistent signal to calibrate its internal clock.

### Day 2: Light Exposure Optimization
Get 10-15 minutes of sunlight within 30 minutes of waking. This triggers cortisol release and sets your circadian clock. No sunglasses ‚Äî your eyes need the light. On cloudy days, still go outside ‚Äî outdoor light is 10-50x brighter than indoor.

### Day 3: Caffeine & Food Cutoffs
- **No caffeine after 2:00 PM** (caffeine has a 6-8 hour half-life)
- **No large meals within 3 hours of bedtime**
- **No alcohol within 3 hours of bedtime** (it disrupts REM sleep)

### Day 4: Screen & Stimulation Management
- **No screens 1 hour before bed** (or use blue light blocking glasses)
- **No work emails or stressful content after 8 PM**
- **No intense exercise within 2 hours of bedtime**

### Day 5: Bedroom Environment Audit
- **Temperature:** 65-68¬∞F (18-20¬∞C) ‚Äî your body needs to cool down to sleep
- **Darkness:** Blackout curtains or sleep mask ‚Äî any light disrupts melatonin
- **Sound:** White noise machine or earplugs ‚Äî consistency is key
- **Bedding:** Quality matters ‚Äî silk pillowcases reduce friction and help skin/hair

### Day 6: Wind-Down Routine Creation
Build a 30-minute routine you do every night:
1. Dim lights in the house (signal to your brain)
2. Light stretching or gentle yoga (5 minutes)
3. Journal or gratitude list (5 minutes)
4. Read fiction ‚Äî not non-fiction, not news (15 minutes)
5. Breathing exercise: 4-7-8 method (5 minutes)

### Day 7: Weekend Consistency
The biggest mistake people make: sleeping in on weekends. Keep your wake time within 30 minutes of your weekday schedule. You can take a 20-minute nap between 1-3 PM if needed ‚Äî but don't shift your anchor.

## 5 Quick Wins (Do These Tonight)

These take less than 10 minutes and most people notice a difference within 48 hours:

### 1. Drop Your Thermostat to 67¬∞F
Your body temperature needs to drop 2-3¬∞F to initiate sleep. A cool room accelerates this process. If you can't control the thermostat, try sleeping with one foot outside the covers ‚Äî it acts as a radiator.

### 2. Put Your Phone in Another Room
Not on silent. Not face down. In another room. The temptation to check it releases cortisol and delays sleep onset by an average of 23 minutes.

### 3. Do the 4-7-8 Breathing Exercise
Inhale for 4 counts. Hold for 7 counts. Exhale for 8 counts. Repeat 4 times. This activates your parasympathetic nervous system and physically slows your heart rate. It's the fastest way to shift from alert to sleepy.

### 4. Set a "Bedtime Alarm"
You have a wake-up alarm. Set a bedtime alarm too ‚Äî 30 minutes before your target bedtime. When it goes off, start your wind-down routine. This one simple habit is more effective than any supplement.

### 5. Write Tomorrow's To-Do List
Take 2 minutes to write down what's on your mind for tomorrow. Research from Baylor University shows this reduces sleep onset time by 9 minutes on average. Your brain can stop "holding" those thoughts and let go.
