Here's what actually matters:

**Sleep Efficiency** = Time asleep √∑ Time in bed √ó 100

- **Above 85%:** You're doing great
- **75-85%:** Room for improvement
- **Below 75%:** Something needs attention

If you're spending 9 hours in bed but only sleeping 7, your efficiency is 78%. The fix isn't sleeping more ‚Äî it's spending LESS time in bed. (I know that sounds backwards.)

**The other number that matters: Sleep Latency**

This is how long it takes you to fall asleep.

- **Under 15 minutes:** Ideal

If your sleep latency is consistently over 30 minutes, the issue is almost always one of three things: timing (wrong bedtime), environment (too warm, too bright), or arousal (anxious/racing thoughts).

## EMAIL 12 (Day 12)

**Subject:** The caffeine lie (it's worse than you think)
**Preview:** Your 2 PM coffee is still in your system at midnight

---

{{first_name}},

How long do you think caffeine stays in your system?

Most people guess 4-5 hours. That's the "half-life" you see on Google.

But half-life means half the caffeine is STILL in your body after 5 hours. And it takes **5 half-lives** to fully clear.

Here's what that actually looks like:

**You drink a coffee at 2:00 PM (200mg caffeine):**
- 7:00 PM ‚Üí 100mg still active
- 12:00 AM ‚Üí 50mg still active
- 5:00 AM ‚Üí 25mg still active

At midnight, you still have the equivalent of half a cup of coffee coursing through your system. And you're wondering why you can't fall asleep.

**It gets worse:** As you age, caffeine metabolism slows. A 25-year-old clears caffeine ~40% faster than a 50-year-old. Same cup of coffee, very different impact.

**My rule:** No caffeine after noon. Not 2 PM. Not 1 PM. Noon.

"But Sarah, I NEED my afternoon coffee."

I hear you. Here's what I recommend instead:

1. **Afternoon slump?** Take a 20-minute nap (set an alarm). More effective than caffeine and no sleep disruption.
2. **Need the ritual?** Switch to decaf after noon. Your brain associates the taste with alertness ‚Äî you'll get a mild placebo effect.
3. **Need actual energy?** 10 minutes of sunlight + a 5-minute walk. Resets your circadian rhythm and gives you 1-2 hours of natural alertness.

## EMAIL 17 (Day 17)

**Subject:** What nobody tells you about melatonin
**Preview:** You're probably taking 10x too much

---

{{first_name}},

Melatonin is the most popular sleep supplement in America. It's also the most misunderstood.

Here's what most people get wrong:

**Myth: "Melatonin is a sleeping pill."**
**Truth:** Melatonin is a *timing signal*, not a sedative. It tells your brain "it's nighttime." It doesn't knock you out.

**Myth: "More is better."**
**Truth:** Most melatonin supplements are 3-10mg. The physiologically effective dose is **0.3-0.5mg.** Ten to thirty times less than what's in the bottle.

Higher doses can actually cause:
- Grogginess the next morning
- Vivid, disturbing dreams
- Headaches
- Your body reducing its own melatonin production

**Myth: "Take it right before bed."**
**Truth:** Take it **60-90 minutes before** your target bedtime. It needs time to build up and signal your circadian system.

**When melatonin actually helps:**
‚úÖ Jet lag (resetting your clock to a new timezone)
‚úÖ Shift work (forcing a new sleep window)
‚úÖ Delayed sleep phase (you're a night owl trying to sleep earlier)

**When it doesn't help:**
‚ùå General insomnia (the problem isn't timing, it's arousal)
‚ùå Waking up during the night (melatonin doesn't maintain sleep)
‚ùå Feeling unrested (that's a sleep quality issue)

**My recommendation:** If you use melatonin, find a **0.3mg or 0.5mg dose** (they exist but you have to look). Take it 90 minutes before bed. Use it for 2 weeks maximum, then stop.

For most people, fixing their light exposure, caffeine timing, and bedroom temperature eliminates the need for any supplement.
