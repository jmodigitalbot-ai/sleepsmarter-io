Hey {{ subscriber.first_name }},

Let me explain what's actually happening when you sleep 8 hours and wake up exhausted.

A full sleep cycle takes about 90 minutes and includes light NREM, deep NREM (also called slow-wave sleep), and REM sleep. You need multiple complete cycles, weighted toward deep NREM early in the night and REM later, to wake feeling genuinely restored.

Poor Recoverers get the quantity — but the distribution is wrong. Too much time in light NREM (Stage 1 and 2), not enough in deep NREM (Stage 3) and REM. You're cycling through the restorative stages too quickly or too shallowly to actually benefit from them.

What causes this? A few interacting factors: core body temperature not dropping sufficiently in the first half of sleep (deep NREM requires your core temp to fall about 1-2 degrees), excess cortisol interrupting slow-wave entry, and sleeping environment cues that keep your brain in light vigilance mode rather than allowing it to fully disengage.

This is why more hours don't help. If your architecture is off, adding an hour of light sleep doesn't add an hour of restoration. You're extending the problem, not fixing it.

The fix requires shifting the distribution — specifically, creating conditions that push your body deeper into Stage 3 earlier in the night, and protecting the REM windows in the second half.
